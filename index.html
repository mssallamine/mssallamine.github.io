Academic Profile

I am a doctoral researcher (2025–2028) at Université Clermont Auvergne, affiliated with the Laboratoire d'Économie d'Orléans (LEO). My thesis, Essays on Macroeconomic Sovereignty in the Global South, is supervised by Prof. Jean-Louis Combes and Prof. Pascale Motel Combes, with academic collaboration in Chad through Prof. Jean-Claude Kouladoum (Université de N'Djaména / LAREMA). I hold a Master 2 in Development Economics from Université Clermont Auvergne (2025) and a Licence in Economics from Kigali Independent University, Rwanda (2021).

Research Focus

My research examines macroeconomic sovereignty in the Global South across four chapters: IMF programme conditionality and developmental fiscal space; global financial cycles and external financing pressure in developing countries; resource dependence, fiscal resilience, and structural transformation; and a geospatial working paper on hydropower and forest loss in Virunga National Park, DRC. I use panel data, event studies, local projections, spatial difference-in-differences, and remote-sensing methods.

Evaluation & Consulting Profile

From February to July 2025, I worked as an intern in the Strategic and Impact Evaluation Department at Aide à la Décision Économique (ADE), Brussels, contributing to EU-funded programme evaluations. I offer consulting support for policy and strategic evaluation, impact evaluation, econometric analysis, geospatial evidence, and reproducible research workflows — working with evaluation firms, NGOs, international organisations, research teams, and public institutions.

International Background

Originally from Chad, I completed my secondary education in N'Djaména before studying economics at Kigali Independent University in Rwanda (2018–2021). I participated in the Aspire Leaders Program at the Aspire Institute, affiliated with Harvard University (2022–2023). I completed graduate studies at Université Clermont Auvergne in France and gained professional experience in Brussels, Belgium.

Research

PhD thesis: Essays on Macroeconomic Sovereignty in the Global South — combining panel econometrics, local projections, event studies, and geospatial methods to study development policy in low- and middle-income countries.

Chapter 1 Preliminary Analysis · Working Paper in Preparation

Debt Sustainability or Developmental Fiscal Space? IMF Programmes and Public Investment in the Global South

Examines whether IMF programme entry is associated with changes in fiscal adjustment, public investment, and developmental fiscal space in low- and middle-income countries. Uses cautious, event-study framing with robustness checks.

Methods & Data Event-study design · IMF MONA · WEO/Fiscal Monitor · ICSD · WDI
Policy Relevance Links fiscal stabilisation to development-oriented public investment in low- and middle-income countries.
Panel Data · Event Study · IMF MONA · Fiscal Policy · Public Investment
Chapter 2 Data Construction · Preliminary Design

Global Financial Cycles and Macroeconomic Sovereignty in Developing Countries

Studies how U.S. rate shocks and global financial conditions affect external financing pressure, external debt exposure, debt-service pressure, reserve buffers, public investment, and macroeconomic policy space in developing countries.

Methods & Data Local projections · U.S. rates · VIX · external debt · reserves · fiscal and investment outcomes
Policy Relevance Connects global monetary conditions to macroeconomic sovereignty and policy space in the Global South.
Local Projections · U.S. Rate Shocks · External Debt · Reserve Buffers · Macroeconomic Sovereignty
Chapter 3 Under Development

Resource Dependence, Fiscal Resilience, and Structural Transformation

Studies how commodity shocks and resource dependence affect fiscal resilience and structural transformation in developing economies, and whether institutions, fiscal rules, and diversification strategies mitigate those effects.

Methods & Data Commodity prices · fiscal indicators · structural transformation measures · institutional and diversification data
Policy Relevance Focuses on resource-dependent economies and long-run development constraints.
Resource Dependence · Commodity Shocks · Fiscal Resilience · Structural Transformation
Chapter 4 Working Paper · Preparing for Journal Submission

Hydropower and Forest Loss in a Conflict-Affected Protected Area: Evidence from the Matebe Project in Virunga

Spatial impact evaluation of the Matebe hydropower project and forest loss around Virunga National Park, DRC. Distinguishes construction and operational phases; results are sensitive to comparison-group choice.

Methods & Data Spatial DiD · event studies · 5 km grid-cell panel · Hansen GFW · ACLED · VIIRS · terrain and road access
Policy Relevance Contributes to debates on energy infrastructure, conservation, and conflict-affected protected areas.
Spatial DiD · Remote Sensing · Deforestation · Hydropower · DRC · Virunga
