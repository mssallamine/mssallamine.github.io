%% ---- WORKING PAPERS & RESEARCH ----------------------------
\cvsection{Working Papers \& Research in Progress}

\begin{tabular}{@{}DC@{}}

Ch.\,1 &
  \textbf{Debt Sustainability or Developmental Fiscal Space?
  IMF Programmes and Public Investment in the Global South}\\
  & \textit{Preliminary analysis. Working paper in preparation.}\\[2pt]
  & Examines whether IMF programme entry is associated with a trade-off between fiscal
    stabilisation and public investment in low- and middle-income countries. Uses programme
    timing from IMF MONA, fiscal variables from WEO/Fiscal Monitor, and investment data from
    the IMF Investment and Capital Stock Dataset (ICSD). Event-study design with additional
    checks on private investment, growth, and HIPC/MDRI debt-relief timing.\\[5pt]

Ch.\,2 &
  \textbf{Global Financial Cycles and Macroeconomic Sovereignty
  in Developing Countries}\\
  & \textit{Data construction and preliminary design.}\\[2pt]
  & Studies how U.S.\ rate shocks, global risk (VIX), and dollar conditions create external
    financing pressures in low- and middle-income countries. Focuses on how external debt
    exposure, reserve buffers, and debt-service pressure condition the erosion of fiscal and
    developmental policy space, including contractions in public investment and growth.
    Local projections framework.\\[5pt]

Ch.\,3 &
  \textbf{Resource Dependence, Fiscal Resilience, and Structural Transformation}\\
  & \textit{Under development.}\\[2pt]
  & Will examine how commodity-price shocks and resource dependence shape fiscal resilience
    and structural transformation in developing economies; whether resource-dependent
    countries are more vulnerable to global shocks; and whether institutions, fiscal rules,
    and diversification strategies mitigate those effects.\\[5pt]

Ch.\,4 &
  \textbf{Hydropower and Forest Loss in a Conflict-Affected Protected Area:
  Evidence from the Matebe Project in Virunga}\\
  & \textit{Working paper. Preparing for journal submission.}\\[2pt]
  & Studies the environmental effects of the Matebe hydropower project near Virunga National
    Park (DRC). Uses a harmonised 5~km grid-cell panel (2010--2020) combining satellite
    forest-loss data (Hansen GFW), population, conflict events (ACLED), nighttime lights
    (VIIRS), terrain, road access, and park-boundary diagnostics. Distinguishes construction
    and operational phases. Spatial difference-in-differences with robustness checks for
    comparison-group sensitivity.\\

\end{tabular}
